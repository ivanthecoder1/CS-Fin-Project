Personal Finance Tips

  • Open a bank account to have a place to safely store your money
  • You can try 50/30/20 budget
  • 50% of your income should be spent on needs like rent, bills, etc
  • 30% of your income should be spent on wants like going out or vacations
  • 20% of your income should be spent on savings like investment or a saving plan
  • These percentages can be changed based on your circumstances and goals. If you want more savings, decrease needs and wants. Ex: You can hold off from going out for dinner or decrease rent by getting a roommate

    • Set up financial goals

  • Use the SMART method to help you plan out your goals
  • Specific (simple, sensible, significant)
  • Measurable (meaningful, motivating).
  • Achievable (agreed, attainable).
  • Relevant (reasonable, realistic and resourced, results-based).
  • Time bound (time-based, time limited, time/cost limited, timely, time-sensitive).

    • Build up your credit score

  • A good credit score will allow you to...
  • Get a mortage
  • Easier asscess to taking out loans
  • Ways to increase credit include:
  • Paying your bills on time
  • Making frequent payments and having multiple active credit cards

    About Us

    WeRise is a educational website designed to connect young POC to important skills such as computer programming and finance, as well as checking in on their mental health. Immediate action is needed in order to diversify the workplace in the future. We created this site in hopes of introducing young people of color, such as ourselves, to new opportunities while nurturing our overall well-being.

    Sydney Chaca

    As a young woman from Latin America, it is disheartening to see a lack of women in tech, and even less WOC. I am determined to use my voice and skills, now and in the future, to encourage and instill hope in other young women to delve into the world of finance and technology.

    Ivan Zhang
